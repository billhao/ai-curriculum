# Data, Curriculum & the Minimum Substrate

Grounding: this note is based on the local `knowledge-vs-intelligence` reports, the existing `interp/open-research-questions.md` list, and a 2025-2026 novelty check against recent arXiv work. I intentionally avoided directly duplicating the standing open questions on RL-from-random-init, wholesale knowledge ablation, or generic architectural decoupling. The proposals below stay in the narrower lane of data, curriculum, and the minimum reasoning substrate.

## Proposal 1: Matched-Entropy Substrate Phase Diagram   [confidence/risk: high novelty, medium confidence, high execution complexity]
- **Overview** — Build a controlled synthetic pretraining suite where total token budget, surface form complexity, and answerable fact count are matched, but the presence of specific substrate primitives is varied: numeracy, state/dependency tracking, comparison/inverse search, symbol remapping, and simple agent-goal/causal structure. Then apply the same tiny post-training packet to every model and estimate each model's reasoning ceiling under increasing test-time compute. The goal is a first empirical "Chinchilla for reasoning substrate": reasoning ceiling as a function of substrate composition, not just parameter count or raw data volume.
- **Motivation** — The local reports converge on "minimum viable substrate = transformable structure, not facts," but that claim is still qualitative. LIMO explicitly states minimal post-training only works when the needed knowledge is already encoded in pretraining ([arXiv:2502.03387](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.03387)); Yue shows current RLVR gains are bounded by the base model at large `k` while distillation can add new patterns ([arXiv:2504.13837](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.13837)); Gandhi shows the relevant base property is the presence of specific reasoning behaviors, and continued pretraining can install them ([arXiv:2503.01307](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.01307)). Recent adjacent work strengthens, but does not settle, the point: Front-Loading Reasoning finds that adding reasoning data earlier in training matters ([arXiv:2510.03264](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.03264)); Interplay isolates pre-training, mid-training, and RL in a controlled synthetic setting and finds RL only helps when pretraining leaves the right headroom ([arXiv:2512.07783](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.07783)); Reasoning Core adds scalable symbolic data but does not isolate facts-vs-structure under matched controls ([arXiv:2603.02208](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.02208)). ARC-AGI 2 provides the cognitive-science anchor: the tasks are supposed to require only core-knowledge priors such as objectness, number, geometry, and goal-directedness, not encyclopedic facts.
- **Hypothesis / Idea** — There is a sparse, non-linear substrate law: downstream reasoning ceiling is largely determined by a small set of transformable primitives, with strong interaction terms rather than smooth additivity. In particular, I expect a threshold effect where `numeracy + state tracking + symbol remapping` causes a discrete jump in downstream reasoning, whereas adding matched "fact-only" data with the same token entropy yields little benefit. If true, "substrate size" should be measurable in primitive-weighted token budget, not in total knowledge bytes.
- **Why it is novel and at the frontier** — Existing work either (a) studies reasoning data added early but not which primitives matter ([arXiv:2510.03264](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.03264), [arXiv:2510.01265](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.01265)), (b) studies symbolic pretraining without matched facts-vs-structure controls ([arXiv:2603.02208](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.02208)), or (c) probes priors at inference time rather than by training counterfactual bases, as in KAAR on ARC ([arXiv:2505.17482](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.17482)). The novelty here is the matched-entropy counterfactual design plus the explicit attempt to fit a scaling law over substrate primitives. That goes beyond the current open-question list, which asks for a characterization of the substrate but does not propose a causal pretraining intervention that would produce one.
- **Proposed experiments** — Use two small-from-scratch models, e.g. GPT-2 `124M` and `355M`, plus one continued-pretraining pilot on an open `0.5B-1.5B` base. Generate corpora for five primitive families and a matched fact-only control, keeping token count, vocabulary size, and narrative surface statistics aligned. Train single-primitive models, all pairwise mixtures, a full-mixture model, and a fact-only control. Then apply the same post-training stack to every checkpoint: `~1K` curated SFT traces in the LIMO/s1 style ([arXiv:2502.03387](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.03387), [arXiv:2501.19393](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.19393)) plus a `~1K` RLVR curriculum on verifiable math/logic/planning tasks. Measure plateau performance rather than single-budget performance by sweeping test-time compute and `pass@k`; evaluate on held-out composition depth, ConceptARC-style transfers, counterfactual analogy, GSM-Symbolic-style perturbations, and a synthetic retrieval-manipulation suite. Fit piecewise power-law or threshold models from primitive token budgets to plateau accuracy. A realistic `1xH800` pilot is the `124M` model family over `6-8` corpora at `100M-200M` tokens each, followed by lightweight SFT/RLVR.
- **Expected outcome** — I expect a small number of primitives to explain most of the variance, with fact-only data underperforming badly once downstream tasks require demo-conditioned transformation instead of recall. If the law is smooth and mostly additive, that still gives a useful substrate metric; if no compact law emerges, that is an equally important negative result because it would imply that the "minimum substrate" picture in the current reports is too low-dimensional.

## Proposal 2: Counterfactual Base/Data Jacobian   [confidence/risk: high confidence on insight value, medium novelty, medium execution risk]
- **Overview** — Instead of comparing opaque public bases, construct a family of nearly identical bases by controlled continued pretraining on modular substrate data, then apply minimal post-training packets one by one. Estimate a response surface, or Jacobian, of downstream capability with respect to pretraining tokens and post-training examples. The point is to answer, cleanly and quantitatively, when "less is more" is actually a statement about the base rather than the fine-tuning data.
- **Motivation** — The strongest empirical clue in the local reports is LIMO's base-swap result: the same `817` examples can differ by `+47.1` AIME depending on the base. Gandhi shows that continued pretraining can rescue a weak base if it installs the right behaviors ([arXiv:2503.01307](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.01307)); Yue argues current RLVR mostly sharpens access to the base model's latent capabilities rather than expanding the frontier ([arXiv:2504.13837](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.13837)); Venhoff shows that steering base models at the right moments can recover most of the thinking-model gap without weight updates ([arXiv:2510.07364](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.07364)). Recent controlled studies are adjacent but not sufficient: Front-Loading finds early reasoning data matters ([arXiv:2510.03264](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.03264)), and Interplay shows RL only yields true gains when pretraining leaves the right headroom and data target the edge of competence ([arXiv:2512.07783](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.07783)). What is still missing is a same-architecture, same-tokenizer, same-checkpoint counterfactual that isolates the marginal contribution of each data type.
- **Hypothesis / Idea** — Post-training packets act mostly as gating functions over latent substrate modules. Their effect is strongly conditional: a packet that teaches manipulation or verification has near-zero value if the corresponding pretraining substrate is absent, then shows a sharp positive derivative once the substrate crosses threshold. I also expect "wrong-answer, right-behavior" traces to work only when the relevant substrate already exists, consistent with Gandhi's behavior finding and inconsistent with a pure imitation story.
- **Why it is novel and at the frontier** — Interplay already provides a controlled synthetic environment for stage-wise reasoning gains ([arXiv:2512.07783](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.07783)), so a vague "let's compare pretraining and RL" proposal would not be novel. The novelty here is the within-checkpoint modular surgery: same base, matched extra tokens, one substrate module changed at a time, then a factorial application of tiny post-training packets. That yields an attributable variance decomposition of "base contribution" versus "packet contribution" and directly operationalizes the reports' "base, not data" thesis rather than merely restating it.
- **Proposed experiments** — Start from a single open base such as `0.5B` or `1.5B`. Produce `6-8` continued-pretraining variants, each receiving equal additional token budgets from one module: facts-only, numeracy, state tracking, knowledge manipulation, error-correction, and symbol remapping, plus a few paired combinations. Then apply small post-training packets: `P1` LIMO-style high-quality verified traces, `P2` long behavior-rich traces with correct behaviors but deliberately wrong final answers, `P3` a small RLVR curriculum, and `P4` a retrieval/tool-use packet. Run a fractional factorial design if full combinatorics are too expensive. Fit a mixed-effects model or Shapley-style decomposition to explain downstream performance by base module, post-training packet, and interaction terms. Evaluate on math, state tracking, self-correction, retrieval-manipulation, and ARC-style remapping tasks, using `pass@k` and test-time compute sweeps to avoid over-indexing on `k=1`. A `1xH800` version is realistic: `0.5B` continued pretraining on `50M-100M` tokens per module, LoRA-based SFT, and short RLVR runs.
- **Expected outcome** — I expect clear phase boundaries: some packets will be ineffective until a substrate module is installed, then suddenly useful. If instead the small packets substitute for missing substrate almost perfectly, that would be a powerful counterexample to the current consensus and would force a rewrite of the local reports' interpretation of LIMO/Yue/Gandhi.

## Proposal 3: Manipulation-First Retrieval Curriculum   [confidence/risk: medium-high confidence, high novelty, medium execution risk]
- **Overview** — Train small models on retrieved micro-worlds where the answer is not directly present in any single retrieved document, so success requires explicit evidence manipulation: compare, classify, inverse-search, update state, adjudicate conflicts, or abstain. Freeze or tightly control retrieval at first so the experiment isolates the reasoner rather than the retriever. The goal is to teach the exact substrate that lets a small model reason over external knowledge instead of merely fetching it.
- **Motivation** — The reports treat Allen-Zhu & Li's knowledge-manipulation result as load-bearing: retrieval is easy, but comparison/classification/inverse search over stored knowledge is hard unless chain-of-thought is present during both training and inference ([arXiv:2309.14402](https://arxiv.org/abs/2309.14402)). Search-R1, Search-o1, and R-Search all improve reasoning-search interaction ([arXiv:2503.09516](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.09516), [arXiv:2501.05366](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.05366), [arXiv:2506.04185](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.04185)), but they are still primarily optimizing search policy and end-to-end QA. KALE improves knowledge manipulation with KG-derived rationales ([arXiv:2601.07430](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.07430)), and Facts-to-Conclusions adds conflict-aware reasoning traces for RAG ([arXiv:2512.16795](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.16795)). None of these isolate the narrow question the reports care about: what curriculum best teaches evidence manipulation itself, independent of retrieval.
- **Hypothesis / Idea** — A manipulation-first curriculum, with typed evidence operators and explicit refusal/conflict behavior, will outperform answer-only RAG fine-tuning and search-policy RL of equal size on multi-hop and conflict-heavy retrieval tasks. The key claim is that once the manipulation substrate is installed, a small model can recover more knowledge-heavy performance from external evidence than standard QA-style post-training predicts, even if its parametric factual recall remains weak.
- **Why it is novel and at the frontier** — This is not another generic search-RL proposal. Search-R1 and R-Search ask how to search better; KALE asks how to internalize KG-based rationales; Facts-to-Conclusions asks how to reason about conflicts inside RAG. The novel part here is to make manipulation operators the curriculum target, hold retrieval controlled, and explicitly train on cases where the answer span is absent and must be constructed. That is much closer to the "small reasoning core + retrieval" thesis in the reports than current open RAG papers.
- **Proposed experiments** — Build a synthetic micro-wiki with entities, attributes, time-stamped revisions, lightweight causal chains, and source conflicts. Generate question families aligned to Allen-Zhu's operator taxonomy plus two extra ones: conflict adjudication and justified abstention. Train four regimes on the same base model (`Qwen2.5-1.5B/3B` or `Llama-3.2-3B`): answer-only SFT, answer-only plus search RL, manipulation-first SFT with typed operator supervision, and manipulation-first SFT followed by search RL. In phase one, freeze retrieval to BM25 or a fixed dense retriever; in phase two, degrade retrieval quality to plot robustness curves. Evaluate on the synthetic suite, on BRIGHT-style retrieval stress, on HotpotQA/MuSiQue-style held-out multi-hop sets, and on MQuAKE-like edited-world tasks where old and new facts conflict. A `1xH800` pilot is straightforward with LoRA or QLoRA and `50K-100K` synthetic training instances.
- **Expected outcome** — I expect manipulation-first training to beat answer-only training under noisy retrieval and conflicting documents, and to fail more gracefully when the retriever is imperfect. If it does not, then the reports are over-weighting knowledge manipulation as the main bottleneck and the real limiting factor is simply better retrieval/search policy.

## Proposal 4: On-Policy Branch-and-Repair Curriculum for Causal Metacognition   [confidence/risk: medium confidence, very high novelty, medium-high execution risk]
- **Overview** — Generate training data from the model's own failed reasoning branches, not from offline perturbations of gold traces. Each example captures a real on-policy error, a diagnosis, a minimal repair, and the continuation after rollback. Train the model to choose when to verify, when to backtrack, and how to make the smallest useful edit, then test whether those behaviors are causally load-bearing rather than merely performative.
- **Motivation** — The reports treat metacognition as an open fault line. Huang et al. argue that LLMs cannot reliably self-correct reasoning without external feedback ([arXiv:2310.01798](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.01798)); Rethinking Reflection in Pre-Training argues that self-correcting behavior emerges during pretraining ([arXiv:2504.04022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.04022)); Synthetic Error Injection Fails shows that perturbing gold traces does not transfer to on-policy self-correction ([arXiv:2512.02389](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.02389)); Step Back to Leap Forward shows explicit self-backtracking can help ([arXiv:2502.04404](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.04404)); SOAR shows models can generate useful stepping-stone curricula under grounded rewards ([arXiv:2601.18778](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.18778)). The local reports' unresolved question is exactly whether metacognition can be taught as data and made causal rather than theatrical.
- **Hypothesis / Idea** — The main failure mode in self-correction data is distribution mismatch: synthetic or gold-trace errors are not the same as the model's own errors. If the curriculum is built from on-policy failure branches and rewarded for minimal successful repair, then verification and backtracking become genuinely useful internal procedures instead of a "wait..." style. I also expect correct-behavior/wrong-answer data to work better when it comes from on-policy branches than from arbitrary synthetic corruptions.
- **Why it is novel and at the frontier** — Adjacent work exists, but the specific combination does not. Rethinking Reflection is observational about pretraining emergence ([arXiv:2504.04022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.04022)); self-backtracking trains search-like behavior ([arXiv:2502.04404](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.04404)); synthetic error injection directly reports failure under off-policy perturbations ([arXiv:2512.02389](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.02389)); ORACLE validates intermediate steps with symbolic supervision in synthetic reasoning data ([arXiv:2603.21140](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.21140)). The novelty here is the on-policy branch distribution plus a causal evaluation criterion for whether metacognitive traces matter.
- **Proposed experiments** — On verifiable tasks such as arithmetic, logic, countdown-style search, and short program synthesis, sample `k` trajectories from the current model, use a verifier to locate the earliest divergence, and then obtain a minimal repair from a stronger teacher or a solver-backed search procedure. Store tuples of `(prefix, wrong branch, diagnosis, rollback point, repaired continuation)`. Build a curriculum over error types: arithmetic slip, omitted constraint, state drift, wrong decomposition, and premature stop. Train with SFT first, then optionally DPO preferring repaired over unrepaired branches, then RLVR rewarding successful repair with a penalty for unnecessary rollback length. Evaluate on seen and unseen error types. For causal evaluation, block or mask the diagnosis/backtrack segment at inference and measure answer drop; force "continue without rollback" and compare to free decoding. A realistic `1xH800` pilot is `1.5B` full fine-tuning or `7B` LoRA over `50K-100K` repair traces plus short RLVR.
- **Expected outcome** — I expect on-policy branch-and-repair data to substantially outperform synthetic error injection on own-error correction and to create a measurable causal dependence on verification/backtracking steps. A negative result would be highly informative: it would suggest that self-correction is mostly an inference-time search phenomenon, not a trainable substrate that data alone can install.

## Proposal 5: Task-Local Symbol-Table Bootstrapping   [confidence/risk: medium confidence, high novelty, medium execution risk]
- **Overview** — Pretrain or continue-pretrain on episodic worlds where the semantics of colors, labels, roles, and objects are remapped every episode over a stable Spelke-like physical substrate. The model must repeatedly infer a fresh local symbol table from a few demos, then reason inside that temporary codebook. This targets a capability that current math-heavy reasoning curricula under-train: overriding global priors and building task-local meaning on the fly.
- **Motivation** — ARC-AGI 2 explicitly identifies task-local symbol grounding as a central failure mode and stresses that models must override pretrained associations to build a new symbol table from demos. KAAR helps by injecting core-knowledge priors in a staged way at inference time ([arXiv:2505.17482](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.17482)); ARCTraj provides temporally ordered human object-level trajectories for ARC tasks ([arXiv:2511.11079](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.11079)); mechanistic work on symbol grounding shows that grounding can emerge in middle-layer computations ([arXiv:2510.13796](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.13796)). The local reports argue that a minimum substrate probably includes objectness, number, space, geometry, and agentness, but current open reasoning datasets are still dominated by math/code and may be missing exactly this remapping skill.
- **Hypothesis / Idea** — Task-local symbol remapping is an independent substrate primitive that transfers to ARC-like and analogy-like reasoning more strongly than an equal token budget of generic math or logic data. If true, some of the apparent "knowledge floor" is really a "task-local grounding floor": the model does not need more facts, it needs more practice constructing ephemeral local semantics.
- **Why it is novel and at the frontier** — KAAR stages priors at inference but does not train the model to construct local symbol tables itself ([arXiv:2505.17482](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.17482)). ARCTraj gives trajectories but not a synthetic pretraining curriculum ([arXiv:2511.11079](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.11079)). Reasoning Core and ORACLE provide broad symbolic data, but their operators usually live in stable formal semantics rather than rapidly changing local ones ([arXiv:2603.02208](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.02208), [arXiv:2603.21140](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.21140)). The novelty is to elevate "infer the codebook for this episode" to a first-class pretraining objective and test whether it is a missing component of the minimum substrate.
- **Proposed experiments** — Create textualized and grid-like episodic datasets where colors, shapes, labels, and action roles are remapped each episode but the underlying object/number/geometry/agent rules stay the same. Include simple object persistence, counting, geometry, and agent-goal tasks, but require the model to first infer the episode-specific mapping from `2-5` demos. Train a `124M` and `355M` from-scratch pilot plus a continued-pretraining version of a `0.5B` base. Add a small post-training stage on textualized ARC solutions and, if useful, ARCTraj-style action traces. Baselines should include equal-token math-only data, generic symbolic data from Reasoning Core, and prompt-time KAAR-style prior injection without training. Evaluate on ConceptARC-style generalization, textual ARC variants, counterfactual analogical reasoning, and held-out remapped vocabularies. A `1xH800` pilot is practical at the `124M-355M` scale with `100M-200M` synthetic tokens plus lightweight SFT.
- **Expected outcome** — I expect a disproportionate gain on tasks that require local meaning override, demo-conditioned abstraction, and symbol grounding, with smaller effects on standard math. If the transfer does not appear, that narrows the bottleneck: ARC-like failures would then look more like search/program-synthesis failures than substrate failures.
